Getting started

Days Since does two things. Events count up: log something you do from time to time — a chore, a habit, a health note, some home maintenance — and see the days since you last did it. Log it again whenever it happens, and the history is kept. Reminders count down: add birthdays, anniversaries, holidays and other dates, and see how many days are left, with optional alerts before the day.

Common questions

  • Is it private? Yes — no account, no ads, no tracking. Your data stays on your device and in your private iCloud.
  • Do I need an account? No. There's no sign-up or login.
  • Does it sync to my other devices? Yes, through your private iCloud — your iPhone and iPad stay in sync automatically.
  • Is there a subscription? No. It's free to start, with a single one-time Days Since Pro unlock for unlimited events and reminders.
  • Can I import from my calendar? Yes — optionally bring in birthdays and recurring dates. Calendar access is used only for import.
  • Why aren't my reminders notifying me? Check that notifications are allowed for Days Since in iOS Settings → Notifications, and that an alert time is set on the reminder.
  • How do I restore Pro on a new device? Open Settings in the app and tap Restore Purchase.

Delete your data

Days Since has no account and no server, so your data is yours to remove directly:

  • Delete individual events or reminders inside the app.
  • Delete the app from your device to remove its local data.
  • Remove the app's iCloud data in iOS Settings (your Apple Account → iCloud → Manage Account Storage) to clear the synced copy across your devices.

Report a problem

If something isn't working — a bug, a sync issue, or a count that looks wrong — please email us with a short description and, if you can, a screenshot. We read every message.
